# Product positioning & quality mandate

Why gruf.io exists and the one guideline that overrides convenience:
**template quality is the product.**

## Market landscape (2026)

Three competitor types, none of which fills the gap gruf.io targets:

1. **Established template sellers** — sell only *curated*, paintable motifs.
   Quality is high, but the catalogue is fixed and the motifs are typically
   kitschy. No custom images.
2. **Custom-painting services** — accept any image, but all run as
   *dropship* fronts on Chinese platforms. Output quality is low; paint
   quality is likely poor, and paints ship only in little pots (dry out
   faster than tubes).
3. **A German "in-house" provider** — claims to produce everything itself,
   but has **no editor**: the customer gets the line-art / linerate version
   *by email* (most likely hand-done in Illustrator; the core business is a
   print shop).

## Our position

gruf.io is the only one combining **custom motif + an interactive editor +
top-tier template quality**. That intersection — a customer's own image,
turned into a genuinely well-made paint-by-numbers template, in a real
editor — is the gap none of the three fills.

## The quality mandate (non-negotiable)

Because the differentiator *is* the template, quality is never a lever we
turn down for speed or simplicity:

- **Maximum paintable detail.** Push every knob to the measured limit of
  what is actually paintable; lose colour/detail only at that limit, never
  earlier. Do not gate detail behind an artificial "number of colours"
  slider — the full Munsell pool is the source of detail.
- **Real, correct colours.** Perceptual colour fidelity end-to-end; the
  palette reduction serves paintability, not a smaller number for its own
  sake.
- **Clean linerate.** Watertight, well-simplified outlines; no over-merged
  regions, no staircase artefacts, labels that fit and read.

This mandate is the *why* behind several standing engineering principles:
build the objectively best result rather than offering trade-off menus, and
never reduce the colour pool early. When a change would trade template
quality for convenience, the default answer is no.
